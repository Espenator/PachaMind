## Executive synthesis

Mallku Aribalo’s two central works frame Andean sacred sites as living initiatory technologies rather than only archaeological remains. "The Awakening of the Puma - Inka Initiation Path" is presented as a work on ongoing astronomical phenomena, ancient wisdom living in stones, the New Pachakuti, Amauta voices, and guidance from Wiraqocha and Pachamama ([Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/27270016-the-awakening-of-the-puma---inka-initiation-path)). The Spanish bibliographic listing identifies the earlier 1997 work as "El despertar del puma: camino iniciático: evidencias astronómicas en los andes," authored by James Arévalo Merejildo Chaski and categorized under Inca astronomy ([Google Books](https://books.google.com/books/about/El_despertar_del_puma.html?id=lFAlAQAAIAAJ)). "Inka Power Places" expands this into a larger system of Andean centers of power, ritual restoration, "cosmic feng shui," and solar initiations, explicitly positioning Mallku’s work as a bridge between archaeology, astronomy, shamanism, and esotericism ([Mallku official page](https://mallku.pro/inka-power-places-book/)).

The academic and heritage record overlaps with Mallku’s work most strongly around the idea that Andean sites integrate religion, ceremony, astronomy, agriculture, and landscape. UNESCO describes Machu Picchu as an "outstanding religious, ceremonial, astronomical and agricultural centre," while noting that its exact astronomical role remains unresolved ([UNESCO World Heritage Centre](https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/274/)). UNESCO describes the Nasca and Palpa geoglyphs as a highly symbolic ritual and social landscape with likely ritual astronomical functions, while recent PNAS research emphasizes pilgrimage, ceremonial use, trail viewing, community ritual, and critiques of constellation-based interpretations as potentially Western projections ([UNESCO World Heritage Centre](https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/700/); [PNAS](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2407652121)). The key dashboard finding is that Mallku bridges ancestral tradition with contemporary seeker paths by converting scholarly themes of ritual, astronomy, sacred landscape, and material culture into experiential practices of invocation, pilgrimage, solar initiation, and personal transformation.

## Core tensions and bridges

| Tension | Where Mallku goes beyond academic interpretation | Where the bridge is credible | Recommended language |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Evidence vs. revelation | Mallku reads sites as encoded messages for initiation and consciousness shift, while scholarship usually treats meaning as partially reconstructable and often unresolved. | The bridge is strongest where academic sources already acknowledge astronomy, ritual, sacred landscape, and pilgrimage, such as Machu Picchu, Nasca, and the ceque system ([UNESCO World Heritage Centre](https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/274/); [UNESCO World Heritage Centre](https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/700/); [Bibliovault](https://www.bibliovault.org/BV.book.epl?ISBN=9780292729018)). | "Mallku offers an initiatory reading of sites that archaeology also recognizes as ritual, astronomical, and landscape-integrated, while scholarship remains more cautious about exact meanings." |
| Archaeo-astronomy vs. esoteric solar science | Mallku uses solar initiation and cosmic feng shui language, which exceeds conventional archaeological claims. | Scholarship supports intentional solar or celestial orientations in some Inca contexts while leaving many functions debated or unresolved ([World History Encyclopedia](https://www.worldhistory.org/Machu_Picchu/); [UNESCO World Heritage Centre](https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/274/)). | "Solar alignment becomes, in Mallku’s teaching, a doorway for personal practice rather than only a calendrical or ceremonial marker." |
| Power plants vs. public education | Mallku’s ecosystem discusses power plants, but modern public-facing materials must avoid sensational, medical, or access-promising claims. | Academic evidence can support historical ritual use of San Pedro, vilca, and Nicotiana without turning that history into a modern promise of results ([PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16625512/); [PMC/PNAS](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12088378/)). | "Plant traditions are part of Andean ritual history and require respect, responsibility, legality, screening, and proper cultural context." |
| Ley lines and geomancy vs. ceque and huaca scholarship | Mallku’s energy-line language is esoteric and experiential; academic work uses social, ritual, calendrical, and political interpretations. | The ceque system provides a grounded comparative anchor because it is a documented sacred line and huaca system radiating from Coricancha ([Bibliovault](https://www.bibliovault.org/BV.book.epl?ISBN=9780292729018)). | "Rather than claiming Western-style ley lines as fact, compare Mallku’s geomancy with Andean ceques, huacas, wakas, and sacred landscape systems." |

## Bottom line

Mallku’s work bridges ancestral tradition and contemporary spiritual seeker paths by turning Andean sites into experiential maps. Academic and heritage sources support the broad substrate: Inca and pre-Inca sacred landscapes combined astronomy, ritual, agriculture, pilgrimage, social order, and ecological dependence ([UNESCO World Heritage Centre](https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/274/); [UNESCO World Heritage Centre](https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/700/); [PNAS](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2407652121)). Mallku’s distinctive contribution is the initiatory overlay: Pachamama and Wiraqocha as living guides, stones as carriers of ancestral memory, solar alignments as personal thresholds, wakas as power places, and the Puma path as awakening rather than only interpretation ([Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/27270016-the-awakening-of-the-puma---inka-initiation-path); [Mallku official page](https://mallku.pro/inka-power-places-book/)).
