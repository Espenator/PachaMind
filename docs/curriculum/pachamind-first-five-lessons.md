## Shared lesson rules

Every lesson must:

- Be free; the only call to action is to **create a free PachaMind learning
  membership** or continue learning.
- Credit **Mallku Aribalo / James Arévalo Merejildo (Chaski)** as tradition-
  holder and Andean shamanic teacher where the tradition is taught.
- Avoid health, medical, therapeutic, cure, or guaranteed-outcome claims.
- Avoid Huachuma/San Pedro conversion hooks, ceremony access, and travel sales.
- Avoid donations, pricing, payments, upgrades, and Embodier.ai commercial
  content.
- Carry the standard educational disclaimer and the recurring message:
  **"No cost, no sales — only learning."**

Safe conversions for these lessons (Ad Grants): lesson started, video watched,
quiz completed, reflection submitted, lesson/module completed, badge earned —
plus free membership created at signup.

## Lesson 1 — What PachaMind Is

- **Slug:** `what-pachamind-is`
- **Risk tier:** Low
- **Goal:** Establish PachaMind as a free Andean wisdom education and cultural
  preservation project, and introduce the free learning membership.
- **Learner outcome:** The learner can explain that PachaMind teaches Andean
  cosmovision for free, preserves culture, and credits its tradition-holder.
- **Key points:** Free, mission-first, commercial-free education; cultural
  preservation and conservation education; Mallku as tradition-holder; the Puma
  Path as a learning progression (not access to anything).
- **Reflection prompt:** What does "learning for its own sake, freely" mean to
  you?
- **Avoid:** Any sales, donor, pricing, or ceremony framing.

## Lesson 2 — Mallku Aribalo: Tradition-Holder and Andean Teacher

- **Slug:** `mallku-aribalo-tradition-holder`
- **Risk tier:** Medium (verify biography, works, and excerpt rights)
- **Goal:** Credit and contextualize Mallku Aribalo / James Arévalo Merejildo
  (Chaski) as tradition-holder and Andean shamanic teacher.
- **Learner outcome:** The learner can identify Mallku as the tradition-holder
  and name themes of his teaching and published works.
- **Key points:** Attribution and respect; teaching themes of Andean sacred
  landscape and cosmovision; works referenced only with verified rights.
- **Reflection prompt:** Why does naming and crediting a tradition-holder matter
  in cultural education?
- **Avoid:** Unverified biography, sensational shaman imagery, outcome promises.
- [NEEDS COUNSEL/VERIFICATION] Rights/permissions for any direct quotation or extended excerpt of Mallku's works.

## Lesson 3 — Kausay Pacha: The Living Universe

- **Slug:** `kausay-pacha-living-universe`
- **Risk tier:** Low
- **Goal:** Introduce Kausay Pacha, the living universe, as the foundation of
  Andean cosmovision.
- **Learner outcome:** The learner can describe the Andean view of a living,
  relational universe as cultural literacy.
- **Key points:** Living-universe framing; relationship and reciprocity as
  cultural concepts; sets up the Three Pachas.
- **Reflection prompt:** Where in your own life do you already practice
  reciprocity?
- **Avoid:** Mystical-proof claims or therapeutic framing.

## Lesson 4 — The Three Pachas: Hanaq, Kay, and Uku

- **Slug:** `three-pachas-learning-map`
- **Risk tier:** Low (archaeo-astronomy elements: Medium — keep academic)
- **Goal:** Present the Three Pachas (Hanaq, Kay, Uku) as a learning map for the
  curriculum.
- **Learner outcome:** The learner can name the three Pachas and what each frames
  (sky/cycles/archaeo-astronomy, reciprocity/daily practice, ancestry/depth as
  cultural integration).
- **Key points:** The Pachas as an educational map; archaeo-astronomy presented
  as scholarly and historical interpretation, not predictive or mystical proof.
- **Reflection prompt:** Which Pacha are you most curious to learn about, and
  why?
- **Avoid:** Overclaiming academic consensus; medical or psychological framing of
  Uku Pacha.

## Lesson 5 — The Five Andean Values

- **Slug:** `five-andean-values`
- **Risk tier:** Low
- **Goal:** Introduce the Five Andean Values — Ayni, Munay, Yachay, Llank'ay, and
  Sami (with Hucha as its counter-concept) — as a values curriculum.
- **Learner outcome:** The learner can name the values and connect them to the
  Puma Path learning progression.
- **Key points:** Ayni (sacred reciprocity), Munay, Yachay, Llank'ay, Sami, and
  Hucha as cultural/educational reflection; values tie into Puma Path badges as
  learning milestones only.
- **Reflection prompt:** Choose one value and describe a small way to practice it
  this week.
- **Avoid:** Self-help hype, therapeutic claims, or framing values as unlocking
  ceremony/travel access.

## Relationship to the curriculum index

These five lessons map to the public, low/medium-risk entries in the
[education content classification matrix](content-matrix.md) lesson index
(e.g., `what-pachamind-is`, `mallku-aribalo-tradition-holder`,
`kausay-pacha-living-universe`, `hanaq-pacha-sky-cycles`,
`ayni-sacred-reciprocity`, `values-munay-yachay-llankay`). The five here are the
recommended first learning loop; deeper lessons continue from the same matrix.
